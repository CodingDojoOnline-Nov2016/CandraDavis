My last post depend on each other, and should be read in sequence. Articles not in bold are optional.

  • Assignment Statment Considered Harmfull. Pervasive mutable state make programs more difficult to deal with. While this is old news, most programmers don't know or don't care. It's a pitty, because simple rememdies exist.
  • Defining Syntactic Sugar. I sometimes hear arguments about what is or ois not syntactic sugar. This is an attempt at defining it.
  • Class-based Programming as Syntactic Sugar. Most of the time, "Object Oriented" actually mean classes. Classes are nothing more than syntactic sugar, at least in statically styped languages such as Java and C++. Sugar is not useless, but seeing past it helps us understand classes better.
  • Taboo "OO". The term "OO" is so overloaded that we should stop using it. Beter substitutes are "classes" and "prototypes".
